Dual-format · W3C VC 2.0 + SD-JWT VC

One claim set. Two interoperable formats. Selective disclosure by design.

A registry of 8 verifiable credential types, each published as a W3C Verifiable Credentials 2.0 JSON-LD credential and an IETF SD-JWT VC — the format frozen into the EU Digital Identity Wallet ARF. The privacy-sensitive claims are individually disclosable, so a holder reveals only what a verifier needs.

Why two formats?

Different ecosystems converged on different containers. Rather than pick a side, every type here is defined once as a JSON Schema claim set and rendered into both — so an issuer can serve a linked-data verifier and an EUDI wallet from the same source of truth. The choosing-a-format guide walks through when to reach for which.
